Execution Protocol

How the Architecture Gets Built.

From intake to delivery, the Architect is in every step. Here is exactly what happens after you click Apply.

Step 01

The Diagnostic

You complete the AI Risk Scanner—a 10-question diagnostic engine. It identifies your exact financial exposure based on active case law and prescribes the correct architectural kit before you pay a single dollar.

Step 02

Payment & Engagement

The moment your kit is prescribed, payment is collected via secure invoice (Wise or MoR) in USD. Because Lex Nova HQ transfers proprietary commercial IP directly to your possession, all sales are final upon delivery.

Step 03

The Vault & SLA Trigger

Payment confirmed. You are redirected to a private, lane-specific intake — The Vault. Here, the Architect collects your exact operating parameters: tech stack, LLM APIs, and data flows. The 48-hour drafting sprint begins the moment this Vault is submitted.

Step 04

Architect Review & Logic Injection

The Architect reviews your Vault parameters personally and custom-injects the legal logic into your documents. Your tech stack, your jurisdictions, your client relationships, your specific risk profile. This is not find-and-replace. This is bespoke architecture built on your exact operating reality.

Step 05

Draft Delivery

Your Review-Ready documents are uploaded to a private Notion Client Portal, accompanied by Architect Notes addressed directly to your local counsel. A personal video walkthrough from the Architect explains the architecture, clause logic, and risk rationale in plain language.

Step 06

Revision Round

You review the architecture. If anything needs adjustment — a clause, a defined term, a jurisdiction-specific nuance — you submit your notes and the Architect revises within 24 hours. One round included in every Kit. Two rounds in every Bundle.

Step 07

Local Counsel Stamp

You take the Review-Ready architecture to your local counsel. They review, validate jurisdictional compliance, and execute. The Architect Notes do the heavy lifting — your counsel stamps, not rebuilds. That is how we kill the billable hour.

Frequently Asked Questions.

Block 1 — The Process

Do you offer custom revisions? ↓
Yes. Every Kit includes one round of async revision. Every Bundle includes two. Revisions are submitted via your Notion Client Portal and completed within 24 hours. Additional revision rounds beyond what is included are available as a Change Order.
What payment methods do you accept? ↓
We invoice globally via Wise (USD bank transfer) and Dodo Payments/Paddle. Payment is 100% upfront for Kits and Bundles. Flagships require a 60% deposit before work begins and 40% on delivery.
What happens after I pay? ↓
The 48-hour drafting sprint begins the moment you submit the Technical Vault. You will receive a Notion Client Portal link confirming your engagement is active. Documents, Architect Notes, and the video walkthrough are delivered directly to that portal.
What jurisdictions do you cover? ↓
We architect for global commercial markets — US, UK, EU, and cross-border operations. Documents are built to international commercial standards. Your local counsel handles the final jurisdictional validation in your specific operating territory.
What if my situation is complex? ↓
Submit your intake and the Architect will review it personally. If your requirements exceed the Kit scope, you will receive a Scope Note explaining exactly what the Kit covers and what requires an upgrade. You will never receive a document that does not match your operating reality.
Is this right for early-stage founders? ↓
Yes. The Kit is specifically designed for founders who are pre-Series A, moving fast, and cannot afford to either leave risk unaddressed or pay Big Law rates to address it. If you have a live product or active client relationships, you need this architecture now — not after your first incident.
What do I do with the documents after delivery? ↓
You take them to your local counsel for final review and execution. The Architect Notes accompanying every document are addressed directly to your counsel and explain the logic, clause rationale, and any jurisdiction-specific considerations. This cuts your counsel's review time dramatically.
Do the documents expire or need updating? ↓
Yes. Laws change, enforcement phases roll out, and case law evolves. The Anchor ($297/month) exists specifically to keep your architecture current. Every Kit client is offered the Anchor on delivery. We recommend it without exception.

Block 2 — Why Lex Nova?

Why not just use a Big Law firm? ↓
Big Law bills by the hour. A senior associate at a top-tier firm runs $800–$1,200 per hour. A standard AI governance review takes 15–20 hours before a single document is drafted. You are looking at $15,000–$25,000 before you have anything executable — and months of back-and-forth while your deals wait. We deliver the same structural sophistication in 48 hours at $1,500. Your local counsel spends one hour validating, not rebuilding. That is the arbitrage.
Why not use a freelance lawyer from Upwork? ↓
A freelance lawyer understands law. They do not understand agentic systems, vector embeddings, retry storms, or UETA attribution. A generic NDA will not protect you from an AI hallucination event. A standard MSA will not survive an enterprise procurement audit. Templates drafted without technical understanding of how your product actually works are not protection — they are exposure dressed as documentation. We architect contracts that match your engineering reality. Upwork cannot do that.
Why not just use ChatGPT to draft the documents? ↓
ChatGPT will produce documents that look correct and read professionally. They will also leave you entirely unprotected because they do not know your specific risk profile, your operating jurisdictions, your tech stack, or your client relationships. Generic output applied to specific risk is the most dangerous position a founder can be in — it creates the illusion of protection without the substance. The Architect builds against your exact operating parameters. That is the difference.
Why the Review-Ready model? Why not deliver final documents? ↓
Because we are not a law firm and we will not pretend to be one. Delivering final executable legal documents without local counsel review would expose you to jurisdictional risk we cannot mitigate from a global practice. The Review-Ready model is not a limitation — it is an honest, commercially superior approach. You get 95% of the work done at a fraction of the cost. Your local counsel does 5% of the work at a fraction of the time. Everyone operates at the top of their capability. That is how the billable hour dies.
