The Reality

Most AI founders don't think about legal architecture until something forces the conversation. A term sheet with a legal due diligence requirement. An enterprise procurement team asking for your DPA. A regulatory inquiry that reveals a gap you didn't know existed.

Your diagnostic suggests you've addressed the catastrophic vulnerabilities. But "better than most" is not the same as "ready for what's next." The gaps that remain don't announce themselves. They show up in the worst possible moment — when a deal is on the line, or when a client's lawyer reads your Terms for the first time.

The Solution

A Lex Nova Gap Review is a focused legal architecture assessment built around your specific diagnostic results. We don't review everything — we go deep on the specific gaps your diagnostic identified and the adjacent vulnerabilities that typically accompany them at your stage.

A traditional legal review bills by the hour and reviews everything. A Gap Review bills by the outcome and reviews only what matters. You are not paying for a lawyer to tell you what your contracts say. You are paying for a Legal Architect to identify exactly what is missing, why it matters, and what the precise fix looks like.

What The Review Covers

Every Gap Review is scoped individually based on your diagnostic results. Depending on your specific gaps, the review may address:

  • ▰ Unenforced consent architecture: Browsewrap terms that are present but legally void under current case standards.
  • ▰ Missing liability language: Standard SaaS liability caps that fail to address autonomous agent actions.
  • ▰ Data processing gaps: Privacy policies that miss AI-specific data flows, model training, or cross-border transfer mechanisms.
  • ▰ IP documentation gaps: Missing Human-in-the-Loop records that leave AI-generated work product legally unownable.
